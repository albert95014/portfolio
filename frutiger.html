Overview
I studied the widely-used typeface Frutiger and its 65 Bold variant, looking into what made the font unique. By capturing the essence of Frutiger letterforms in a three-by-three grid, I expanded upon my research by looking into its usages and history to show them in a motion graphics video.

Studying Frutiger Letterforms
To research Frutiger, I looked into what it was used for and why it was known as the most "general used typeface." I found that certain characteristics made it very legible from many distances, such as strong verticals, thick yet elegant curves, and large apertures. Using this info, I made numerous iterations of a three-by-three grid consisting of parts of Frutiger letters to try and emphasize what makes the typeface special.

Creating a Video
After studying Frutiger's letterforms, I created a motion graphics video that discusses and shows Frutiger's usage and history. I created a lo-fi storyboard in Illustrator to plan out the different scenes, then used AfterEffects to make my video.
